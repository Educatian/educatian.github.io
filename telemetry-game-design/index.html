Telemetry Design for Educational Games

What to log before your learning game becomes impossible to study. A visual onboarding guide for turning gameplay into defensible evidence without confusing clicks, struggle, hints, sensors, or dashboards with learning itself.

Game logs ECD Stealth assessment MMLA Troubleshooting Public-safe templates
Core rule

Do not log everything. Log the observable behavior and game state needed to support the claim you intend to make.

01 · Purpose

Educational games fail as research instruments when logging is added last.

A game can be engaging, beautiful, and still impossible to study. If the only exported fields are final score, completion, and time, later analysis cannot reconstruct strategy, support use, failure recovery, misconception patterns, or ethical reasoning.

Underlogging

Only saves score, completion, and total time. Process evidence disappears.

Overlogging

Records every click but not the game state, available choices, task meaning, or support context.

Instrumented design

Maps learning claims to game mechanics, observable actions, structured events, and cautious interpretations.

04 · Event families

Log by game mechanic, not by screen widget.

TeachPlay

Design task started, AI disclosure edited, prototype evidence uploaded, feedback revised, reflection submitted.

Cyber_Grid

Node inspected, route rotated, trace run, threat port identified, quarantine attempted, debrief submitted.

Shadow Packet

Evidence inspected, NPC interviewed, Packet Lens opened, evidence linked, resolution submitted, misconception flagged.

AI Ethics Voyage

Council scenario opened, choice selected, rationale entered, stakeholder consequence viewed, compass guidance accessed.

05 · MMLA case

When logs are not enough, add modalities carefully.

Moon et al. tracked distress during educational gameplay by fusing gameplay logs with facial-expression data. The lesson is not that every game needs cameras. The lesson is that affective and embodied claims require evidence beyond raw game actions or much stronger contextual logging.

MMLA adds interpretive power and methodological burden: synchronization, sensor quality, privacy, missing data, and uncertainty.
Use MMLA when

The construct is affective, collaborative, embodied, or strategic and gameplay logs alone cannot distinguish likely interpretations.

Do not use it when

The claim can be supported by structured gameplay events, or raw media would create unnecessary privacy risk.

Log the quality

Record modality availability, sync offset, missingness, and confidence. A missing sensor stream is data about the study design.

06 - YouTube embed tutorial

Embed videos only when they carry the evidence logic.

This guide embeds one topic-matched YouTube video because it explains the assessment design idea behind educational game telemetry: gameplay actions become useful evidence only when the target claims, observable behaviors, and interpretation rules are planned in advance.

Embedded video

Valerie Shute, "Stealth assessment: embedded evidence-based assessment in games." Watch it for the ECD logic: define the competency, identify observable gameplay evidence, and design tasks so logs can support a defensible interpretation.

Source archive and session note

Why not embed loosely related videos?

A motivational game-based learning talk may be interesting, but it does not automatically explain telemetry design. Embed only videos that directly clarify logging, evidence, assessment, multimodal traces, privacy, or validity.

08 - Troubleshooting

When your logs are already messy, diagnose the failure mode.

Most telemetry problems are recoverable only if you know which assumption failed: time, identity, state, meaning, privacy, or versioning.
SymptomLikely causeHow to troubleshoot
Events are out of orderClient clock drift, async writes, offline upload.Sort by sequence_index; keep server receipt time separate.
Same action has different meaningsGame state was not logged.Add pre_state, post_state, mission_id, and available_actions.
Hint analysis is misleadingHint use is confounded with prior struggle.Rebuild around pre-hint attempts, errors, elapsed time, and recovery.
Time-on-task is inflatedIdle time, tab switching, classroom interruptions.Log focus/blur, pause/resume, inactivity thresholds, and active windows.
MMLA streams do not alignVideo/audio/sensor start times differ from game time.Add sync markers and store sensor start offsets.
Model accuracy looks high but uselessLeakage from outcome or post-outcome events.Train only on features available before the prediction point.
09 - Privacy and retention

More sensitive data needs a stronger reason.

Low

Pseudonymous gameplay events with no free text or media. Usually enough for sequence, support-use, and completion analyses.

Moderate

Free-text rationale, open response, or derived feature that may reveal sensitive information. Needs careful export rules.

Sensitive

Raw audio, video, face, gaze, biometric, exact location, or directly identifying data. Collect only when the claim needs it.
